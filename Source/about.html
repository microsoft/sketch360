Sketch 360

A Microsoft Garage Project

With Sketch 360, you can create 360 degree panoramic sketches from a single point looking in all directions. 360 cameras save these spherical images in a flat JPEG images by using an equirectangular projection and added specific metadata to the images identifying them as 360 photos. Sketch 360 saves files in the same way so the sketches can be viewed as 360 photos on sites like Facebook, Adobe Lightroom.com, and Kuula.co.

Using Sketch 360

Sketch 360 has two primary surfaces, a drawing canvas, and a 360 view. You draw on the canvas with a pen or touch and every time a stroke is added, the 360 view is updated, rotating to the end point of the ink stroke. At any time you can rotate the 360 view with your finger to see other views of the sketch.

With a device that supports a stylus like the Microsoft Surface Duo or the Samsung Galaxy Note, you draw with the pen and manipulate the position and zooming of the canvas with your touch. On non-stylus aware devices, you can switch to touch-drawing mode to interpret finger touches as pen or eraser strokes instead of panning and zooming. In addition to using a pinch gesture to zoom, you can use the zoom slider.

If using a stylus with an eraser end like the Surface Pen, you can erase any stroke by dragging the stylus over it. If you are using touch, then press the eraser button to switch to erasing mode.

To help with connecting the left and right edges of a drawing in the 360 view, you can see a little bit of the opposite drawing edge when drawing close to the left or right edge of the drawing canvas.

In the 360 view, press the tilt & rotate button to enable the device's gyroscopic sensor to determine the camera's tilt and rotation.

Pen Color and Thickness

To the side of the drawing canvas, you can tap on the color button or the thickness button to change the color or thickness of the ink that is created.

Undo and Redo

Any drawing or erasing can be undone or redone easily by tapping the undo and redo buttons.

Drawing View and 360 View

Switch to the drawing view or 360 view modes to only show one of the two panes. Navigate back in the app to go back to the two-pane view.

Orthogonal Guidelines

Turning on the orthogonal guidelines helps you draw lines that align with the primary axes in the equirectangular grid. If you are facing the center point of the drawing canvas.

  • Red lines go from your front to your back.
  • Blue lines go from your left to your right.
  • Green lines go from the top to the bottom.
  • The Two-point stencil helps you draw lines intersecting any two points. To position it, drag the two circles to reposition them and the draw the line over the dashed stencil curve. Menu

  • New sketch: erase the current sketch and create a new blank one.
  • Open sketch: open a .sketch360 file that was previously saved to the device.
  • Save sketch: save the current drawing to a .sketch360 file on the device.
  • Export image: save the current drawing to a .jpeg image with 360 metadata on the device.
  • Share: Share the sketch as a .jpeg image with 360 metadata to other apps on the device.
  • Sketch properties: change the name and background color of the sketch.
  • Settings: change the grid opacity, the orientation of the two surfaces, the pixel size of exported images, and to reload the example sketch shown on first launching the app.

About Sketch 360

Sketch 360 was originally created by Michael Scherotter and released as free app built for Microsoft Surface in the Windows Store in 2018 to help him create 360 sketches. Here are some examples of sketches he drew with it using Microsoft Surface Pro and Surface Go tablets. Michael has been sketching and keeping journals for thirty years ever since he started studying architecture in college. He made his career in software development, with a specific passion for building creative tools but never stopped sketching. Sketch 360 for the Android was built by Microsoft employees, Michael Scherotter, Matthew Leibowitz, Shane Neuville, and Komal Priya in company-wide hackathons run by the Microsoft Garage.
